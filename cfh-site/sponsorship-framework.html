1. Purpose and Principle

The previous version of this document was built for a schools context, where CFH acted as a formal gatekeeper — reviewing business applications, coordinating with PTAs, and conducting ongoing monitoring. That model made sense for schools, where CFH carries significant responsibility for what reaches children and their families.

Sports clubs are different. Club committees are made up of adults who know their local community, their members, and their values. They are well-placed to make sound judgments about which businesses are appropriate to associate with their club. Requiring every business to go through a formal CFH vetting process would be bureaucratic, slow, and frankly unnecessary.

This document reflects that reality. Responsibility for sponsor suitability is shared — with CFH doing the groundwork upfront, and clubs exercising judgment at the club level.

2. How Responsibility Is Divided

CFH is responsible for…The Club is responsible for…
Providing the Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines to every club at onboarding

Filtering the AI business list to exclude prohibited categories (tobacco, adult content, predatory lenders, MLMs)

Applying conditional logic to the AI list (alcohol and gambling businesses appear only for adult-membership clubs)

Responding to any concern raised about a specific business on the platform

Maintaining and updating these guidelines as the platform evolves		 Reading and understanding the Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines before beginning outreach

Applying good judgment when approaching businesses from their own network

Considering their membership composition when assessing conditional categories (alcohol, gambling)

Declining to proceed with any sponsor whose values clearly conflict with the club's own

Notifying CFH if a concern arises about a sponsor relationship

3. Club Quick-Check — Businesses From Your Own Network

When a club approaches a business that isn't on the CFH AI list, run a quick mental check. This is not a formal submission process — it's a prompt to use good judgment.

4. No Formal Business Application Required

Under the previous vetting model, businesses were required to submit a Sponsorship Application Form before a club could approach them. This process no longer applies for sports clubs.

Businesses do not apply to CFH. Clubs approach businesses. There is no application form, no formal CFH review, and no approval workflow before outreach begins. The safeguards are:

  • The Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines, provided to clubs upfront
  • The AI business list, pre-filtered by CFH to exclude prohibited categories
  • Club judgment, applied at the local level by people who know their community

This is a deliberate design choice. CFH is not a regulator. We are a platform that helps clubs fundraise well. Treating every local business like a formal applicant would undermine the speed and simplicity that makes CFH useful.

6. Note on Schools Phase (Future)

When CFH extends to schools in Phase 2, this framework will not apply. Schools will operate under a more formal process — closer to the original vetting model — reflecting CFH's greater responsibility in a school environment. Alcohol, gambling, and other conditional categories will be excluded entirely.

Clubs that are also affiliated with school programmes should apply the stricter schools-phase standards to any school-facing campaigns, even during Phase 1.
