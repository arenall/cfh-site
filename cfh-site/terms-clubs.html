1. Introduction and Parties

These Terms of Service ('Terms') govern the relationship between Community Fundraising Hub ('CFH') — a Charitable Trust Board incorporated under the Charitable Trusts Act 1957 (Incorporation No: 70000374, NZBN: 9429053669445) — and each sports club or sporting organisation ('Club', 'you', 'your') that registers with and uses the CFH platform.

By completing CFH's club intake form, registering your Club, or using CFH services, you agree to these Terms on behalf of your Club and confirm that you have authority to bind your Club to them.

These Terms should be read alongside the CFH Privacy Policy and the CFH Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines and Sponsorship Responsibility Framework (provided at onboarding). Together, these three documents form the complete agreement between your Club and CFH.

3. CFH's Services to Clubs

3.1 Custom Club Landing Page

CFH will build a custom-branded fundraising webpage for your Club campaign displaying your name, logo (if provided), campaign description, sponsorship tiers, and campaign progress. The page will be hosted by CFH for the duration of the campaign. CFH reserves the right to review and edit copy for accuracy, tone, or compliance with these Terms.

3.2 AI-Generated Business List

CFH will provide your Club with a tailored list of local businesses researched using AI tools. Please note:

  • Business details (names, addresses, categories) should be independently verified by the Club before contact, as AI-generated information may contain errors, outdated details, or misidentified business categories
  • The list is not a guarantee of any business's interest, availability, or willingness to donate
  • The list is screened against the Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines on a best-efforts basis — this is not a warranty that every business on the list is suitable. The Club must apply its own judgment
  • The list is for Club use only and must not be shared commercially or used for any purpose other than the Club's CFH campaign

Reaching out to businesses is entirely the Club's responsibility. CFH does not contact businesses on the Club's behalf.

3.3 Outreach Templates

CFH will provide a suite of personalised outreach templates (email, WhatsApp, social media formats) designed to be sent from the Club's own contact channels.

3.4 Automated Donation Processing and Receipting

CFH receives all donations on behalf of the Club as the charitable trust, issues donation tax receipts to donors (once donee status is confirmed), records all donation data, and provides the Club with a campaign summary and donor acknowledgement list.

3.5 Settlement — Weekly

CFH will transfer campaign proceeds to the Club's nominated bank account weekly, net of the Platform Fee and Payment Processing Fee, with a settlement summary. Settlement is currently performed manually. CFH may adjust settlement frequency with minimum 14 days' written notice. Disputes about settlement amounts must be raised within 14 days of the settlement notification.

3.6 End-of-Campaign Impact Report

At the conclusion of each campaign, CFH will provide a summary impact report including total raised, number of sponsors, and tier breakdown.

4. Club Registration and Eligibility

4.1 Eligibility

To register with CFH, a Club must be a legitimately operating sports club or sporting organisation in New Zealand, have a designated contact person with authority to enter this agreement, have a NZ bank account, and agree to these Terms and the Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines. CFH reserves the right to decline any Club registration.

4.2 Club Information and Bank Account Changes

The Club is responsible for providing accurate and complete information throughout the campaign. Changes to the Club's nominated bank account must be submitted in writing to hello@cfh.org.nz and take effect only upon CFH's written acknowledgement. CFH accepts no liability for settlement to an incorrect account where the Club failed to follow this process.

5. Charitable Status and Tax Receipts

Community Fundraising Hub is incorporated as a Charitable Trust Board under the Charitable Trusts Act 1957. Our application for registration with Charities Services NZ is currently under assessment (as at May 2026).

Important — Current Status of Donee Status and Tax Receipts

Until CFH receives its donee status number (DT number) from IRD, donations through the CFH Platform do NOT qualify for the NZ donor tax credit.

Clubs MUST NOT represent donations as tax-deductible or promise tax receipts to businesses or individuals. Making such a representation before donee status is confirmed may breach the Fair Trading Act 1986 and could expose the Club and CFH to liability. CFH can issue a donation confirmation only — not a tax receipt.

Once donee status is confirmed, donors will receive automated tax receipts from CFH within 48 hours of donation. All donations through the CFH Platform are donations to Community Fundraising Hub as the charitable trust — CFH then applies those funds to support the named Club's community purposes.

6. Platform Fee, Payment Processing, and Settlement

6.1 Platform Fee — 7%

CFH retains a Platform Fee of 7% from all Donations processed. This fee is applied to each donation at the time it is received, is not subject to GST (CFH is not currently GST registered), and is non-negotiable during the current Phase 1 period — except where a trial or pilot arrangement has been agreed in writing (see Section 6.5). CFH will give at least 30 days' written notice if its GST status changes.

6.2 Payment Processing Costs

Stripe's payment processing fees are deducted from each donation before settlement. Current rates are published at stripe.com/en-nz/pricing. CFH will notify Clubs of any material change to its payment processing provider.

6.3 Settlement

CFH will settle campaign proceeds weekly, net of all fees. Disputes about settlement amounts must be raised within 14 days of the settlement notification.

6.4 Refunds

CFH may issue a refund within 7 days of the donation where a reasonable basis exists (for example, an accidental duplicate donation). If a refund is issued, the settlement amount to the Club is reduced accordingly. If CFH is required to hold funds pending an AML/CFT investigation, settlement for those specific funds will be suspended for up to 30 days.

6.5 Trial and Pilot Campaigns

CFH may from time to time offer trial or pilot campaigns on varied fee terms (including at no cost). Any such variation will be confirmed in writing to the Club before the campaign begins and supersedes Section 6.1 for that campaign only. All other Terms continue to apply in full during a trial or pilot campaign.

7. Club's Responsibilities and Conduct

7.1 Ethical Sponsorship Compliance

The Club is responsible for ensuring that all businesses it approaches are consistent with CFH's Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines.

Absolute Prohibitions — NEVER permitted on CFH
  • Tobacco, vaping, and smoking-related products
  • Adult content
  • Predatory lenders (payday lenders, high-cost credit providers targeting vulnerable borrowers)
  • Multi-level marketing (MLM) schemes and pyramid selling
  • Businesses engaged in illegal activity in New Zealand
  • Gambling (except permitted conditional categories for adult sports clubs per the Guidelines)

7.2 No Tax Receipt Representations Before Donee Status

Clubs must not represent donations as tax-deductible or promise tax receipts to any business or individual until CFH has notified the Club that donee status has been confirmed by IRD.

7.3 Accuracy of Campaign Information

The Club must ensure all campaign information is accurate, including how raised funds will be used, the Club's status and activities, sponsorship benefits offered, and CFH's role as platform provider and charitable vehicle (not a co-endorser of Club activities).

7.4 Club's Responsibility for Sponsor Relationships

The relationship between the Club and any business sponsor is the Club's own. CFH is not a party to any agreement between the Club and a specific business, does not guarantee any business will donate, and is not responsible for the Club's fulfilment of sponsorship commitments.

7.5 Use of CFH Materials

Outreach templates, AI business lists, and other CFH materials are licensed to the Club solely for use in connection with its CFH campaign. The Club must not share, resell, or distribute these materials, or use CFH's name, logo, or brand in a manner not authorised by CFH.

7.6 Compliance with NZ Law

The Club is responsible for ensuring its use of CFH's platform complies with all applicable NZ laws, including the Privacy Act 2020, Fair Trading Act 1986, Charities Act 2005, and any sport-specific regulations.

7.7 Notification of Material Changes

The Club must promptly notify CFH of any change of club contact or bank account details, dissolution or restructuring, any legal proceeding or regulatory action, or any concern about a sponsor's suitability.

8.1 CFH's Obligations

CFH will build and host the Club Landing Page, provide the AI Business List within the agreed timeframe, process donations promptly and accurately, issue donor tax receipts once donee status is active, transfer settlement proceeds weekly net of fees, maintain campaign data securely, and provide a campaign summary at the end of each campaign.

8.2 No Guarantee of Fundraising Outcomes

CFH does not guarantee any specific fundraising outcome. CFH provides no warranty regarding the amount the Club will raise, the number of donors who will contribute, or any commercial outcome.

8.3 Platform Availability

CFH will take reasonable steps to ensure the Club Landing Page is accessible during the campaign but does not guarantee uninterrupted access and accepts no liability for loss caused by platform downtime, technical issues, or third-party service failures.

10. Suspension and Termination

10.1 Club's Right to Withdraw

A Club may withdraw at any time by written notice to hello@cfh.org.nz. Upon withdrawal, the Club Landing Page will be taken down, outstanding settlement amounts will be transferred after deduction of all fees and refunds, and donation records will be retained by CFH as required by law.

10.2 CFH's Right to Suspend or Terminate

CFH may suspend or terminate a Club's access without notice if the Club has provided false or misleading information, is in breach of these Terms or the Ethical Sponsorship Guidelines, CFH becomes aware of a prohibited sponsor on the Club's campaign, or the Club is subject to legal proceedings or regulatory action. For other concerns, CFH will provide written notice and a 7-day opportunity to respond.

10.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination: outstanding donations are settled net of fees; CFH retains donation records as required by law; the Club must immediately cease using CFH materials and branding.
